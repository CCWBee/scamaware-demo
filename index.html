Know the threats

Learn to recognise the most common scams targeting Jersey residents.

Bank Impersonation

Texts or calls claiming your account is compromised. They'll ask you to move money to a "safe account" — which belongs to the scammer.

Check if it's a scam →

Delivery Fee Scams

A text claims you need to pay £1.99 to receive a parcel. The link captures your card details and drains your account.

Check if it's a scam →

Investment Fraud

Promises of guaranteed high returns on crypto or overseas investments. If it sounds too good to be true, it always is.

Check if it's a scam →

Tax Scams

Calls claiming to be from Treasury & Exchequer about unpaid taxes, threatening immediate arrest unless you pay now.

Check if it's a scam →

Romance Scams

Fake relationships built over weeks, ending with urgent requests for money. Jersey loses over £400k to these annually.

Tech Support Scams

Calls claiming your computer has a virus. They request remote access, then steal your data or install malware.

Red flags to watch for

Scammers rely on urgency and emotion. Pause and look for these signs.

⚠

Urgency & Pressure

"Act now or your account will be closed" — legitimate organisations don't threaten you like this.

💳

Unusual Payment Methods

Gift cards, cryptocurrency, or wire transfers. No real company asks for payment this way.

🔒

Password or PIN Requests

Your bank will never ask for your PIN or full password. Not by phone, not by text. Ever.

🤫

"Keep This Secret"

Scammers isolate their victims. If they tell you not to tell anyone, tell someone immediately.

🎁

Too Good to Be True

Won a lottery you didn't enter? Unexpected inheritance from abroad? It's always a scam.

💻

Remote Access Requests

Never let anyone you don't know take control of your computer. This gives them everything.
